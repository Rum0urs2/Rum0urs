Research and Publications

Atmospheric Science & Climate Projections

Research Overview

My research focuses on the identification of large-scale circulation and thermodynamic drivers of heatwaves using ERA5 reanalysis data, aimed at improving forecasting reliability and understanding climate extremes.

In addition to heatwave dynamics, I develop multi-model mean frameworks designed to enhance precipitation accuracy in regional climate models. This work bridges the gap between complex atmospheric physics and actionable climate intelligence, specifically focusing on the South Asian monsoon and tropical variability.

Primary Themes

Heat-Wave Forecasting

Leveraging high-resolution ERA5 data to isolate the precursor signals and thermodynamic signatures that drive persistent high-temperature events.

Climate Projections

Advancing the credibility of long-term climate scenarios for India through optimized multi-model ensembles and bias correction techniques.

Precipitation Accuracy

Developing robust multi-model mean frameworks to mitigate uncertainties in seasonal precipitation forecasting and extreme rainfall events.

Large-scale Circulation

Analyzing the interaction between global circulation patterns and regional weather extremes to improve sub-seasonal to seasonal predictability.
